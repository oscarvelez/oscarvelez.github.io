Dodge Challenger

The Dodge Challenger, compared to its similar American counterparts, only has 3 generations, starting out in 1979.

The latest generation rivals the current Ford Mustang and Chevrolet Camaro.

The first generation was built similar to the Plymouth Barracuda using the same "Chrysler E Platform", designed for rear wheel drive cars.

The second generation was a more compact car, and had smaller engines




Generation 1

From 1970 to 1974 was Dodge's answer to its competitor, the Mustang and Camaro.

Assembled in the US, this 2 door muscle car was a big hit for young Americans.

Available in both a hardtop coupe or convertible design, and equipped with a V8 engine.

Generation 2

After a 4 year hiatus the Challenger was revived for the 1978 model year until 1983.

Assembly was done in Japan alongside the Mitsubishi Galant Lambda, which resembles the Challenger

After the second generation, it wasn't until 25 years later in 2008 when Dodge brought it back

Generation 3

The latest generation was brought back with inspiration from the 1970 model.

This generation was an instant success with models including: R/T, SRT8, SRT Hellcat.

Models include a V6 or V8 engine, with the higher ends boasting a HEMI V8 engine,

with 0-60 speeds as fast as 2.3 seconds!
